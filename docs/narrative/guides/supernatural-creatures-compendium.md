## Monster Taxonomy Framework

### Classification by Origin

#### **Mythological**
- From ancient religious texts and epic tales
- Often tied to gods and cosmic forces
- Epic scale and legendary status
- **Examples**: Hydra, Minotaur, Phoenix, Jörmungandr

#### **Folklore**
- From local legends and cautionary tales
- Tied to specific locations or cultural fears
- More intimate and regional
- **Examples**: Wendigo, Banshee, Kappa, Chupacabra

#### **Modern Fiction**
- Created in contemporary media (books, games, movies)
- Reflects modern anxieties
- Creator-defined rules
- **Examples**: Clickers (*Last of Us*), Dementors (*Harry Potter*), Titans (*Attack on Titan*)

### Classification by Type

#### **Corporeal** (Physical Body)
- Can be wounded by physical means
- Has biology, needs sustenance
- Leaves corpse when killed
- **Storytelling Use**: Direct physical combat, tactical challenges

#### **Ethereal** (Spiritual/Incorporeal)
- No physical body, cannot be harmed by normal weapons
- Passes through walls, may be invisible
- Requires magic/faith to combat
- **Storytelling Use**: Psychological horror, mysteries, spiritual themes

#### **Shapeshifter** (Variable Form)
- Changes physical appearance
- Multiple forms with different abilities
- Identity and trust become central themes
- **Storytelling Use**: Paranoia, mystery, social horror

#### **Elemental** (Composed of Element)
- Made of fire, water, earth, air, etc.
- Clear weaknesses and immunities
- Environment becomes part of combat
- **Storytelling Use**: Environmental challenges, elemental warfare

#### **Construct** (Artificially Created)
- Doesn't reproduce naturally
- Created through magic, science, or hybrid
- Questions of free will and purpose
- **Storytelling Use**: Creator responsibility, sentience debates

### Classification by Behavior

#### **Hunter/Stalker**
- Intelligent predator
- Tracks prey methodically
- Often plays with victims
- **Examples**: Predator, Xenomorph, classic Dracula

#### **Swarm/Horde**
- Overwhelming numbers
- Individual weakness, collective strength
- Mindless or hive-minded
- **Examples**: Zombie hordes, Tyranids, insect swarms

#### **Intelligent/Mastermind**
- Human-level or greater intelligence
- Plans and schemes
- Can be negotiated with (maybe)
- **Examples**: Liches, Elder Vampires, Dragons, Mind Flayers

#### **Mindless/Instinctive**
- Acts on simple programming
- Predictable patterns
- Environmental hazard more than character
- **Examples**: Gelatinous Cube, mindless zombies, oozes

### Classification by Horror Element

#### **Body Horror**
- Grotesque physical transformation
- Violation of human form
- Disease and mutation
- **Examples**: The Thing, Flood (Halo), Cronenberg monsters

#### **Psychological Horror**
- Attacks sanity and mind
- Creates paranoia and doubt
- May not be physically dangerous
- **Examples**: Mind Flayers, SCP-style entities, psychological ghosts

#### **Existential Horror**
- Questions reality and meaning
- Incomprehensible to human mind
- Often tied to cosmic horror
- **Examples**: Lovecraftian entities, reality-benders

## Undead

### General Undead Characteristics
- Once living, now animated by magic or curse
- Often vulnerable to holy power
- May be mindless or retain intelligence
- Often tied to necromancy

### Zombies

#### **Classic Zombie** (Romero-style)
- **Origin**: Recent dead reanimated
- **Behavior**: Slow, mindless, hungry for flesh/brains
- **Transmission**: Bite/scratch infection
- **Weaknesses**: Head destruction, fire
- **Swarm Nature**: Dangerous in numbers
- **Examples**: *Night of the Living Dead*, *The Walking Dead*

#### **Fast Zombie** (Rage Virus)
- **Origin**: Viral infection while living
- **Behavior**: Aggressive, fast, feral
- **Transmission**: Bloodborne pathogen
- **Weaknesses**: Still mortal, bleed out
- **Examples**: *28 Days Later*, *World War Z* (movie)

#### **Undead Horde Variations**:
- **Draugr** (Norse): Intelligent, super-strong, guard tombs
- **Revenants**: Return for specific purpose (usually revenge)
- **Wights**: Corporeal undead with some intelligence, often armed
- **Ghouls**: Flesh-eaters, often living in graveyards
- **Skeletons**: Animated bones, no flesh, immune to pierce/slash

### Vampires
*(See dedicated Vampires section below)*

### Liches

**Definition**: Undead spellcasters who achieved immortality through dark magic

**Creation Process**:
- Powerful wizard performs dark ritual
- Transfers soul into phylactery (soul jar)
- Body becomes undead while retaining full intelligence
- Nearly impossible to kill permanently (must destroy phylactery)

**Characteristics**:
- Retain all magical knowledge
- Continue magical research
- Often skeletal or partially decomposed
- Powerful necromantic abilities
- Centuries or millennia old

**Phylactery**:
- Contains lich's soul
- Can be any object
- Hidden in secure location
- Lich regenerates if phylactery intact

**Variants**:
- **Archlich**: Good-aligned lich (very rare)
- **Demilich**: Ancient lich, only skull remains
- **Baelnorn**: Elf lich guarding sacred places

**Examples**:
- *D&D*: Acererak, Vecna
- *Adventure Time*: The Lich
- *World of Warcraft*: Kel'Thuzad

### Mummies

**Origin**: Preserved corpse animated by curse or ritual

**Types**:
- **Egyptian Mummy**: Cursed pharaohs, protect tombs
- **Bog Mummy**: Preserved in peat, nature magic
- **Cursed Mummy**: Animated by specific curse

**Abilities**:
- **Mummy Rot**: Curse/disease from touch
- **Dust Form**: Dissolve and reform
- **Curse**: Often connected to disturbing tomb
- **Command Insects**: Desert creatures

**Weaknesses**: Fire, removal of wrappings

**Examples**:
- *The Mummy* (1999): Imhotep
- *D&D*: Mummy lords

### Wraiths & Specters

**Definition**: Incorporeal undead spirits

**Wraith**:
- **Form**: Dark, shadowy humanoid
- **Abilities**: Life drain, shadow step, phase through walls
- **Weakness**: Sunlight, holy magic
- **Origin**: Died with powerful negative emotions

**Specter**:
- Similar to wraith but less powerful
- Often bound to location of death
- Can possess living

**Shade**:
- Weakest form of shadow undead
- Echo of person who died
- Limited intelligence

**Examples**:
- *Lord of the Rings*: Nazgûl/Ringwraiths
- *Harry Potter*: Dementors (functionally similar)
- *D&D*: Wraith, specter, shadow

### Death Knights

**Definition**: Undead warriors, often paladins who fell from grace

**Characteristics**:
- Retain combat prowess from life
- Dark paladin abilities
- Command undead
- Mounted on undead steeds
- Often bound to necromancer or lich

**Examples**:
- *Warcraft*: Arthas Menethil
- *D&D*: Death knights (cursed paladins)

### Other Undead Types

**Banshee**:
- Female spirit, wails to predict death
- Irish folklore origin
- Ear-splitting scream can kill
- Often tied to specific family

**Poltergeist**:
- Noisy, mischievous spirit
- Throws objects, slams doors
- Often tied to living person's psychic energy
- Not always malevolent

**Dullahan**:
- Headless horseman
- Irish mythology
- Carries own head
- Omen of death

**Draugr**:
- Norse undead
- Guard burial mounds
- Super strength
- Can increase in size
- *Skyrim*: Common enemy

## Spirits & Ghosts

### Ghost Types

#### **Residual Haunt**
- Recording of past event
- No consciousness or awareness
- Repeats same action endlessly
- Cannot interact with living
- **Example**: Ghost replaying own death

#### **Intelligent Ghost**
- Conscious spirit
- Can communicate and interact
- Aware of current events
- May or may not know they're dead
- **Motivations**: Unfinished business, revenge, attachment to place/person

#### **Poltergeist**
- Violent, chaotic manifestation
- Often tied to living person (usually teen with psychic energy)
- Throws objects, causes chaos
- May not be actual ghost but psychokinetic phenomenon

#### **Vengeful Spirit** (Onryō)
- Died with powerful grudge
- Seeks revenge on living
- Often extremely dangerous
- Japanese tradition: long black hair, white clothing
- **Examples**: *The Ring* (Sadako), *Ju-On: The Grudge* (Kayako)

### Spirit Entities

**Elemental Spirits**:
- Embodiment of natural forces
- Neither good nor evil inherently
- Can be summoned/bound
- Fire spirits (Salamanders), Water spirits (Undines), etc.

**Nature Spirits**:
- Dryads (tree spirits)
- Naiads (water spirits)
- Oreads (mountain spirits)
- Often protective of their domain

**Ancestral Spirits**:
- Spirits of the dead watching over descendants
- Can provide guidance or protection
- Honored in many cultures

**Shadow People**:
- Dark humanoid figures
- Seen in peripheral vision
- May be interdimensional beings
- Modern paranormal phenomenon

## Demons & Infernal Entities

### Demon Hierarchy

#### **Lesser Demons**
- **Imps**: Small, mischievous, shape-shifters
- **Lemures**: Mindless blob-like demons
- **Quasits**: Tiny demons, often familiars
- **Characteristics**: Weak individually, numerous

#### **Greater Demons**
- **Balor**: Massive, flaming, whip-wielding
- **Marilith**: Six-armed serpentine demon
- **Pit Fiend**: Ultimate devil form (Lawful Evil)
- **Characteristics**: Ancient, powerful, lead demon armies

#### **Demon Lords**
- **Orcus**: Demon Prince of Undead
- **Demogorgon**: Prince of Demons, two-headed
- **Baphomet**: Minotaur demon lord
- **Characteristics**: Demi-god level, rule layers of Abyss/Hell

### Devil vs Demon (D&D Distinction)

**Demons** (Chaotic Evil):
- From the Abyss
- Chaotic, destructive
- No hierarchy, constant infighting
- Pure destruction and chaos

**Devils** (Lawful Evil):
- From the Nine Hells
- Strict hierarchy
- Make contracts
- Temptation and corruption through deals

### Demon Types by Culture

#### **Christian Demonology**
- 72 demons of the *Ars Goetia*
- Named demons: Asmodeus (lust), Beelzebub (gluttony), Mammon (greed)
- Seven Princes of Hell (seven deadly sins)
- Fallen angels

#### **Islamic Demonology**
- **Djinn/Jinn**: Beings of smokeless fire, free will
- **Ifrit**: Powerful, malevolent djinn
- **Ghoul**: Flesh-eating desert demon
- **Shayatin**: Evil spirits, tempters

#### **Japanese Oni**
- Ogre-like demons
- Red or blue skin, horns, fangs
- Wield iron clubs
- Can be tricked despite strength

#### **Hindu Asuras**
- Powerful beings opposed to Devas (gods)
- Not inherently evil, but often antagonistic
- Complex morality

### Demon Abilities (Common)
- Possession (taking control of human body)
- Deals/Pacts (granting power for souls)
- Shapeshifting
- Fire/shadow manipulation
- Immortality (must be banished, not killed)
- Corruption (turning good to evil over time)

### Demonic Possession Signs
- Change in voice
- Knowledge of hidden things
- Speaking languages never learned
- Supernatural strength
- Aversion to holy symbols
- Sudden personality change
- Levitation

**Exorcism**: Ritual to drive out possessing demon
- Requires faith and authority
- Often dangerous to exorcist and possessed
- Multiple sessions may be required

## Angels & Celestial Beings

### Angel Hierarchy (Christian Tradition)

#### **First Sphere** (Closest to God)
- **Seraphim**: Six wings, surround throne, constant worship
- **Cherubim**: Guardians, often depicted with multiple faces
- **Thrones**: Living symbols of God's justice

#### **Second Sphere** (Heavenly Governors)
- **Dominions**: Regulate angel duties
- **Virtues**: Bestow blessings, perform miracles
- **Powers**: Warrior angels, fight demons

#### **Third Sphere** (Messengers/Guardians)
- **Principalities**: Guide nations and cities
- **Archangels**: Chief messengers (Michael, Gabriel, Raphael, Uriel)
- **Angels**: Guardians of individuals

### Angel Characteristics
- Immortal, ageless
- Powerful warriors
- Holy/radiant energy
- Often incomprehensible true forms ("Be not afraid")
- Can take human appearance
- Bound by divine law
- Cannot lie (in many traditions)

### Fallen Angels
- Rebelled against God/heaven
- Cast out and became demons
- Lucifer/Satan as leader
- Retain angelic power but corrupted
- **Examples**: Lucifer, Azazel, Belial

### Nephilim
- **Origin**: Children of angels and humans
- **Characteristics**: Half-angel, half-human
- **Abilities**: Supernatural strength, long life, holy powers
- **Status**: Often considered abominations
- **Examples**: *Supernatural*, *Shadowhunters*

### Other Celestial Beings

**Valkyries** (Norse):
- Choosers of the slain
- Guide worthy warriors to Valhalla
- Beautiful warrior women
- Ride winged horses

**Apsaras** (Hindu/Buddhist):
- Celestial dancers and musicians
- Incredible beauty
- Often sent to seduce mortals/ascetics

**Devas** (Hindu/Buddhist):
- Celestial beings, god-like but not omnipotent
- Inhabit higher planes
- Can be reborn in lower realms

## Shapeshifters & Lycanthropes

### Werewolves

**Transformation Triggers**:
- **Classic**: Full moon (involuntary)
- **Controlled**: At will (experienced werewolves)
- **Emotional**: Anger, fear, stress
- **Hybrid**: Maintain intelligence in wolf form

**Types**:
- **Cursed**: Involuntary, struggle with beast
- **Born**: From werewolf parent, more control
- **Bitten**: Infected, painful transformations
- **Skinwalker**: Native American witch, voluntary

**Werewolf Abilities**:
- Enhanced strength, speed, senses (even in human form)
- Rapid healing (except silver wounds)
- Pack telepathy (in some traditions)
- Extended lifespan

**Weaknesses**:
- Silver (burns/poisons)
- Wolfsbane/aconite
- Decapitation
- Sometimes vulnerable only during full moon

**Pack Dynamics**:
- Alpha, beta, omega hierarchy
- Pack bonds and loyalty
- Territorial
- Enhanced when fighting together

**Examples**:
- *Underworld*: Lycans vs Vampires
- *Teen Wolf*: Pack dynamics
- *The Howling*, *An American Werewolf in London*

### Other Werebeasts (Lycanthropes)

- **Werebear**: Strong, protective, often good-aligned
- **Weretiger**: Solitary, fierce hunters
- **Wereboar**: Aggressive, tough
- **Wererat**: Urban, sneaky, often evil
- **Werespider**: Rare, web-spinning

### Shapeshifters (Non-Lycanthrope)

**Kitsune** (Japanese):
- Fox spirits
- Gain tails with age/power (up to nine)
- Shapeshifts into human (often beautiful woman)
- Trickster or protector
- Foxfire magic

**Selkie** (Celtic):
- Seal people
- Wear seal skin to become seal
- If skin stolen, trapped in human form
- Often tragic love stories

**Skinwalker** (Navajo):
- Witch who can become any animal
- Wears animal hide
- Malevolent
- Taboo to speak about

**Doppelgänger**:
- Exact duplicate of living person
- Evil omen
- Can replace original
- Kills and takes over life

**Mimic**:
- Shapeshifts into objects (usually treasure chests)
- Ambush predator
- Adhesive to trap prey
- *D&D* classic

**The Thing**:
- Assimilates and perfectly imitates organisms
- Paranoia incarnate
- Any cell can regenerate entire organism
- Fire is primary weakness

## Vampires

### Classic Vampire Traits
- Immortal, ageless
- Drink blood to survive
- Enhanced strength, speed, senses
- Rapid healing
- Attractive/seductive
- Cannot enter home without invitation
- Create new vampires through bite/blood exchange

### Vampire Weaknesses (Traditional)
- **Sunlight**: Burns/kills
- **Stake through heart**: Kills
- **Decapitation**: Kills
- **Holy symbols**: Repels
- **Garlic**: Repels
- **Running water**: Cannot cross
- **Need invitation**: Cannot enter homes uninvited
- **Reflection**: Don't have one
- **Counting**: Compelled to count small objects (some traditions)

### Modern Vampire Variations

**Sunlight-Resistant**:
- *Twilight*: Sparkle in sunlight
- *Daywalker* (*Blade*): Immune to sunlight
- *True Blood*: Drink synthetic blood

**Emotional/Psychic Vampires**:
- Feed on life force/emotions instead of blood
- Can walk in daylight
- Often more subtle

**Viral Vampires**:
- *Blade*, *I Am Legend*: Scientific/viral origin
- More zombie-like, less intelligent

### Vampire Society

**Hierarchy**:
- Ancient elders (centuries/millennia old)
- Vampire lords/ladies
- Fledglings (newly turned)
- Progeny (created by specific vampire)

**Courts & Clans**:
- Political structures
- Territorial disputes
- Masquerade (hiding from humans)
- Different clans with different abilities (*Vampire: The Masquerade*)

**Examples**:
- *Dracula*: Original novel, elegant noble
- *Underworld*: Vampire vs Lycan war
- *Castlevania*: Dracula and vampire aristocracy
- *Vampire: The Masquerade*: Complex political RPG

## Fey & Fairies

### Fey Courts

**Seelie Court** (Summer, Light):
- Generally more benevolent (but still dangerous)
- Beautiful and radiant
- Honor-bound
- Queens: Titania, Mab (in some traditions)

**Unseelie Court** (Winter, Dark):
- Malevolent and cruel
- Dark and terrifying
- No honor, pure chaos
- Queens: Queen of Air and Darkness

**Neither**:
- Many fey belong to neither court
- Independent spirits
- Follow own rules

### Fey Characteristics
- Immortal or extremely long-lived
- Bound by complex rules and bargains
- Cannot lie (but can mislead)
- Names have power (never give true name)
- Capricious and dangerous
- Iron is anathema (burns/poisons)
- Time moves differently in fey realm

### Types of Fey

**Pixies/Sprites**:
- Tiny, winged
- Mischievous pranksters
- Minor magic
- Can turn invisible

**Brownies**:
- Household helpers
- Offended by direct payment
- Leave if insulted
- Scottish folklore

**Redcaps**:
- Malevolent goblin-like fey
- Dye caps in victim's blood
- Wear iron boots
- Scottish borders

**Changelings**:
- Fey child left in place of stolen human baby
- Often sickly or strange
- May not know their true nature

**Leprechaun**:
- Irish fairy
- Hoard gold
- Grant wishes if caught
- Master of trickery and escape

**Dryad**:
- Tree spirit
- Bonded to specific tree
- Dies if tree is destroyed
- Shy, protective of forest

**Banshee**:
- (Also listed in undead, as classification varies)
- Wails to predict death
- Attached to families
- Irish tradition

## Dragons & Dragonkin

### True Dragons

**Characteristics**:
- Intelligent, often genius-level
- Hoard treasure (gold, knowledge, art)
- Breath weapon (fire, ice, acid, lightning, poison)
- Scaled armor (resistant to magic)
- Flight (even with seemingly impossible wingspan)
- Long-lived (thousands of years)
- Shapeshifting (in some traditions)

**Age Categories** (D&D model):
- Wyrmling (youngest)
- Young
- Adult
- Ancient
- Great Wyrm (eldest, most powerful)

**Chromatic Dragons** (Evil):
- **Red**: Fire, greed, arrogance (strongest)
- **Blue**: Lightning, desert-dwelling, organized
- **Green**: Poison gas, forest-dwelling, manipulative
- **Black**: Acid, swamp-dwelling, cruel
- **White**: Ice, arctic, least intelligent but vicious

**Metallic Dragons** (Good):
- **Gold**: Fire, wisest, most powerful
- **Silver**: Ice, noble, protectors
- **Bronze**: Lightning, coastal, curious
- **Copper**: Acid, tricksters, jovial
- **Brass**: Fire, desert, talkative

**Gem Dragons** (Neutral):
- Psionic abilities
- Crystalline scales
- Neither good nor evil

### Dragon Variations

**Eastern Dragons**:
- Serpentine, no wings
- Control weather
- Benevolent, wise
- Associated with water
- Celestial beings
- **Example**: *Spirited Away* (Haku)

**Wyverns**:
- Two legs (not four)
- Tail stinger (often venomous)
- Less intelligent than true dragons
- Often used as mounts

**Drakes**:
- Flightless dragons
- Four legs, no wings
- Strong and aggressive
- Lesser intelligence

**Lindworms**:
- Serpentine, no legs or minimal legs
- Often venomous
- Nordic tradition

### Dragonborn/Half-Dragons

- Humanoid with dragon features (scales, breath weapon, tail)
- Born from dragon/human union or draconic magic
- Inherit some dragon abilities
- Mortal lifespan (extended)

**Examples**:
- *Skyrim*: Dovahkiin (Dragonborn)
- *D&D*: Dragonborn race
- *Game of Thrones*: Targaryens (dragon blood)

- Winged horse

**Centaur**:
- Half-man, half-horse
- Some wise (Chiron), others savage
- Archers and warriors

**Harpy**:
- Woman's head, bird body
- Filthy, steal food
- Wind spirits

### Norse Mythology

**Fenrir**:
- Giant wolf
- Son of Loki
- Prophesied to kill Odin at Ragnarök
- Bound by gods with magical chain

**Jörmungandr**:
- World Serpent
- So large it encircles Earth
- Battles Thor at Ragnarök
- Poisonous

**Sleipnir**:
- Eight-legged horse
- Odin's mount
- Fastest of all horses
- Born from Loki

**Nidhogg**:
- Dragon that gnaws at roots of Yggdrasil
- Eats corpses in Náströnd

### Egyptian Mythology

**Sphinx**:
- Lion body, human head
- Guardian of temples
- Riddle-giver
- Greek version has wings

**Anubis**:
- Jackal-headed god
- Guide of dead
- Weighs hearts against feather of Ma'at

**Ammit**:
- Crocodile, lion, hippopotamus hybrid

- The Devourer

### Japanese Mythology

**Tengu**:
- Bird-like demon/spirit
- Long nose or beak
- Martial arts master
- Mountain dwellers

**Kappa**:
- Water demon
- Child-sized, turtle-like
- Dish of water on head (source of power)
- Loves cucumbers

**Tanuki**:
- Raccoon dog spirit
- Shapeshifter
- Trickster, often comedic
- Large testicles (traditional depiction)

**Jorogumo**:
- Spider woman
- Seduces men, then eats them
- Can control other spiders
- Creates illusions

## Horror Creatures

### Body Horror

**The Thing** (Assimilator):
- Imitates any organism perfectly
- Spreads at cellular level
- Any part can regenerate whole
- Fire vulnerability
- Ultimate paranoia fuel

**The Flood** (*Halo*):
- Parasitic life form
- Converts all sentient life
- Hivemind intelligence
- Forms grotesque combat forms
- Exponential growth

**Necromorphs** (*Dead Space*):
- Reanimated corpses
- Grotesque transformations
- Created by Markers
- Dismemberment required (headshots insufficient)

**Xenomorph** (*Alien*):
- (Excluded per user request, but noting as reference point for comparison)

### Psychological Horror

**SCP Foundation Entities**:
- Anomalous objects and creatures
- Varying threat levels
- Often incomprehensible
- Require special containment
- **Examples**: SCP-173 (statue that moves when not observed), SCP-682 (unkillable reptile)

**Slender Man**:
- Tall, faceless humanoid in suit
- Causes paranoia and memory loss
- Targets children
- Cannot be photographed clearly
- Modern internet-born legend

**Weeping Angels** (*Doctor Who*):
- Quantum-locked (only move when not observed)
- Touch sends victim to past
- Stone statue appearance
- "Don't blink"

### Viral/Infected

**Clickers** (*The Last of Us*):
- Fungal infection (Cordyceps)
- Echolocation via clicking sounds
- Blind but extremely dangerous
- Eventual progression to bloaters

**Rage Zombies** (*28 Days Later*):
- Viral infection causes extreme aggression
- Fast, relentless
- Still technically alive
- Starve to death eventually

**The Crossed** (*Garth Ennis*):
- Virus removes all inhibitions
- Extremely violent and creative with cruelty
- Retain intelligence
- Cross-shaped rash on face

## Swarm & Horde Creatures

### Insect Swarms

**Locusts**:
- Biblical plague
- Devour all vegetation
- Blot out the sun
- Unstoppable tide

**Spiders**:
- Venomous bites
- Web entrapment
- Giant versions (fantasy)
- Swarm of small ones

**Wasps/Hornets**:
- Aggressive when provoked
- Painful stings
- Can chase for long distances
- Nest defense

### Undead Hordes

**Zombie Apocalypse**:
- Overwhelming numbers
- Relentless advancement
- Noise attracts more
- Society collapse
- **Scenarios**: *Walking Dead*, *World War Z*, *Left 4 Dead*

**Skeleton Army**:
- Animated by necromancer
- Silent, coordinated
- No morale issues
- Often armed with weapons from life

### Alien Swarms

**Tyranids** (*Warhammer 40K*):
- Extragalactic hivemind
- Devour all biomass
- Adapt rapidly to threats
- Countless bioforms
- Unstoppable tide

**Zerg** (*StarCraft*):
- Hivemind insectoid aliens
- Rapid evolution and adaptation
- Assimilate genetic material
- Overwhelming numbers

## Storytelling Applications

### Monster as Metaphor
- **Zombies**: Mindless consumerism, conformity, pandemic
- **Vampires**: Aristocracy, addiction, immortality's curse, sexual predation
- **Werewolves**: Loss of control, dual nature, puberty, rage
- **Demons**: Internal struggles, addiction, trauma
- **Eldritch Horror**: Insignificance, incomprehensible universe

### Building Dread
- Unknown monster (unseen threat)
- Escalating encounters (glimpses before reveal)
- Evidence of past victims
- Monsters that break expected rules
- Unkillable antagonists

### Encounter Design
- **Swarm**: Resource attrition, feeling overwhelmed
- **Hunter**: Cat and mouse, suspense
- **Intelligent**: Psychological warfare, traps
- **Environmental**: Hazard navigation

### Moral Complexity
- Monsters with sympathetic origins
- Humans more monstrous than monsters
- Monsters protecting something
- Cursed monsters seeking cure
